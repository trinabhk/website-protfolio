The person reading your robots.txt for fun.

A BCA student from Nepal who got pulled into the technical side of search and stayed there.

I'm Trinabh Karki, a BCA student from Nepal working as an SEO Analyst at SmartSites Nepal. My work focuses on technical SEO, indexing, crawlability, search engine visibility, and resolving the issues that stop important pages from showing up properly in search.

I work regularly with Google Search Console, Bing Webmaster Tools, Screaming Frog, Google Analytics, and different CMS platforms. A lot of my day is spent checking indexable and non-indexable pages, fixing canonical and noindex issues, reviewing robots.txt, submitting URLs for indexing, and validating fixes after implementation.

On the implementation side, I handle hands-on updates across WordPress, Shopify, Webflow, Magento, Wix, Squarespace, BigCommerce, Joomla, MODX, Sanity, Weebly, Square, static websites, headless CMS setups, and cPanel-based websites. I also work with domain and hosting platforms like GoDaddy, Bluehost, WP Engine, and other providers for DNS, TXT records, HTML file uploads, meta tag verification, and CMS-based verification.

I also run technical audits with Screaming Frog, prepare campaign and quarterly reports, find redirect chains, broken links, 404s, 5xx issues, crawl errors, alt text issues, and recurring Google Search Console problems. After fixes are made, I validate them in Search Console and track whether the affected pages recover.

How I think about the work

Good technical SEO is mostly about removing friction between a site and a crawler. A page that returns the right status code, declares its canonical, and shows up in a valid sitemap is a page Google can trust. I like that the work is verifiable - you can watch a URL move from Discovered to Indexed and know the fix held.

This portfolio runs on the same principles I apply to client sites: clean URLs, valid metadata, a real sitemap, and pages that are actually indexable.
