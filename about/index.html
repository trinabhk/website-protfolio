The person reading your robots.txt for fun.

A BCA student from Nepal who got pulled into the technical side of search and stayed there.

I'm Trinabh Karki, a BCA student from Nepal working as an SEO analyst at SmartSites Nepal. My job sits right where technical search engine optimization meets front-end web work - and most of my day goes into fixing the code and configuration issues that quietly keep websites from ranking on Google.

On the front-end, I do hands-on updates across WordPress, Shopify, Wix, Squarespace, and BigCommerce. I've worked with Shopify Liquid, and I regularly manage the things that decide how a page is treated in search: title and meta tags, canonical URLs, noindex rules, and robots.txt structure. I also run audits to track down and repair broken links - the 3xx redirects and 4xx errors that leak crawl budget and frustrate users.

The other half of my work is setup and verification. I handle the technical groundwork to launch or verify a site, working directly with DNS configurations and TXT records across hosts like GoDaddy, Cloudflare, WP Engine, and HostGator, so properties verify cleanly in Google Search Console.

How I think about the work

Good technical SEO is mostly about removing friction between a site and a crawler. A page that returns the right status code, declares its canonical, and shows up in a valid sitemap is a page Google can trust. I like that the work is verifiable - you can watch a URL move from Discovered to Indexed and know the fix held.

This portfolio runs on the same principles I apply to client sites: clean URLs, valid metadata, a real sitemap, and pages that are actually indexable.
